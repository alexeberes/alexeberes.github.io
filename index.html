About Me

My name is Alex Beres. I'm currently a freshman studying biomedical engineering at Northwestern University. I am an undergraduate research assistant in the Leonard Lab for Cellular Devices and Biomolecular Engineering. I'm especially interested in how synthetic biology can be used to treat human diseases.

Outside of school, I enjoy reading, running, playing soccer, programming and traveling the world. I've also competed in and won a few hackathons; my projects can be found on my Github and Devpost. Right now I'm reading Immune by Philipp Dettmer. I've reviewed a few of the most recent books I've read here.

I love exploring new landscapes, cultures, and countries and meeting interesting people from different backgrounds. Some of my favorite places I've been include Thessaloniki, Olympia, and Athens, Greece; Suzhou and Shanghai, China; and Tome, Tokyo, and Kyoto, Japan.

If you want to talk about my research, reading, traveling, or anything else, please reach out!
