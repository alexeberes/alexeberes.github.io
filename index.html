About Me

My name is Alex Beres. I'm currently a freshman studying biomedical engineering at Northwestern University. I am also an undergraduate researcher in the Leonard Lab for Cellular Devices and Biomolecular Engineering. I'm especially interested in how synthetic biology can be used to treat human diseases.

Outside of school, I enjoy reading, running, playing soccer, programming, and traveling the world. I've competed in and won a few hackathons. My projects can be found on my Github and Devpost. I'm currently reading Immune by Philipp Dettmer. I've reviewed a few of the most recent books I've read here!

I love exploring new landscapes, cultures, and countries and meeting interesting people from different backgrounds. Some of my favorite places I've been to include Thessaloniki, Olympia, and Athens, Greece; Suzhou and Shanghai, China; and Tome, Tokyo, and Kyoto, Japan.

If you want to talk about my research, reading, traveling, or anything else, please reach out!

Research

Mammalian Synthetic Biology

I am currently an undergraduate researcher in the Leonard Lab at Northwestern working with doctoral student Hailey Edelstein. My work focuses on mammalian synthetic biology, specifically, synthetic transcription factors and receptors. I hope to use what I learn through this research to work on the development of engineered cell therapies.

Regenerative Engineering

I am also a part of the Competition for Undergraduate Regenerative Engineering at the Northwestern Center for Advanced Regenerative Engineering. As part of the Adipose-Derived Stem Cell team, my focus is on the development of an adipose-derived stem cell-seeded hydrogel to regenerate tissue in diabetic wounds.
